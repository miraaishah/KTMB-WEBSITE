Regional Commuter Rail

KTM Komuter Northern

Affordable, scenic, and convenient daily rail transit across Penang, Kedah, Perlis, and Perak.

What is KTM Komuter Northern?

The **KTM Komuter Northern** (Komuter Utara) is the primary regional passenger rail transit system connecting communities across the four northern states of Peninsular Malaysia. Launched in 2015, it has dramatically simplified transport for daily workers, shoppers, and university students commuting between rural townships and mainland industrial areas.

This network operates two highly utilized transit routes centered around the Butterworth Sentral transportation hub. Tickets are extremely affordable, with local fare brackets starting at less than RM 2.00, enabling tourists to travel from Penang to the Thai border cheaply.

Key Transit Benefits

1
Penang Ferry Connection

Direct pedestrian sky-bridge access between Butterworth station and the Penang Sentral ferry terminal.

2
Scenic Route Travel

Enjoy breathtaking scenic views of Kedah's signature green paddy fields and Bukit Merah's lake track.

3
Border Gateway Access

Terminates at Padang Besar station, allowing easy walks to custom counters for Thailand entry.

Northern Train Lines

Two regional lines spanning northern Peninsular Malaysia.

Butterworth - Padang Besar Line

Serving Penang mainland, central Kedah (Alor Setar), Arau (Perlis), and terminating at Padang Besar.

Butterworth - Padang Rengas Line

Connecting Penang mainland to southern Kedah and northern Perak townships like Taiping and Padang Rengas.
