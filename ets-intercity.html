What is ETS & KTM Intercity?

The **Electric Train Service (ETS)** is Malaysia's fastest intercity passenger rail network, operating at speeds of up to 140 km/h on the electrified double-track line. It connects Gemas in the south to Padang Besar in the north, passing through major economic hubs like Kuala Lumpur, Ipoh, and Butterworth (Penang).

**KTM Intercity** serves as the vital traditional diesel locomotive network linking remote towns along the East Coast (Jungle Railway) from Gemas to Tumpat, as well as providing localized international transfers via the Shuttle Tebrau between Johor Bahru and Woodlands (Singapore).

Key Station Highlights

Major stations served by the ETS and Intercity rail network.

Padang Besar (Perlis)

Northern terminus on the Thai border, offering passport checkpoints and shuttle services into Hat Yai.

Butterworth (Penang Sentral)

Direct connectivity to the Penang Ferry service, taking passengers straight into Georgetown.

Ipoh (Perak)

Historic heritage train station serving Perak's food and culture capital.

Kuala Lumpur (KL Sentral)

The central transportation hub of Malaysia, connecting all transit networks, LRT, MRT, and airport lines.

Gemas (Negeri Sembilan)

The intersection connecting the electrified West Coast Line and the diesel East Coast Line.

Johor Bahru (JB Sentral)

The southern gateway terminal connecting passengers directly to Woodlands, Singapore.
