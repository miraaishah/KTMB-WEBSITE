KTM Komuter Klang Valley

Connecting housing suburbs, educational institutions, and busy commercial centers in Kuala Lumpur.

What is KTM Komuter Klang Valley?

Launched in 1995, the **KTM Komuter Klang Valley** network is a crucial passenger suburban train service serving Kuala Lumpur and the surrounding Selangor districts. Operating on dual electrified lines, it provides hundreds of thousands of commuters with an affordable, congestion-free alternative to reach city jobs.

KTM Komuter integrates with the wider RapidKL transit system (LRT, MRT, and Monorail) at interchange hubs such as KL Sentral, Kajang, Kampung Baru, and Subang Jaya. Tap-and-go capabilities with Touch 'n Go cards and Komuter Link cards make ticketing seamless.

Key Transit Benefits

1
High Passenger Capacity

Each six-car train can hold up to 1,100 passengers during rush hour traffic.

2
Ladies Coach Protection

Dedicated coaches (Coach E & F) designated exclusively for female passengers for enhanced safety and comfort.

3
Cashless Convenience

Support for standard Touch 'n Go card tapping and KITS QR boarding passes at automated ticket gates.

Transit Features & Accessibility

Practical services designed for everyday commuters and students.

👩

Ladies Coach

Middle coaches E and F are strictly for female riders. Look for pink platform markings.

♿

Accessibility

Wheelchair ramps, dedicated coach seating areas, and tactile paths at major stations.

🚲

Foldable Bikes

Commuters are allowed to bring folded bicycles onboard during off-peak hours for last-mile transit.

❄️

Air-Conditioned

Fully air-conditioned coaches ensure a cool, relaxing commute despite outdoor heat.

Klang Valley Train Lines

Two major lines serving the central rail corridors.

Batu Caves - Pulau Sebang Line

Connects the northern tourist attraction Batu Caves to Tampin/Pulau Sebang in Negeri Sembilan, crossing KL Sentral.

Tanjung Malim - Port Klang Line

Connects Tanjung Malim in southern Perak to the Port Klang coastal terminal, passing through Rawang and Shah Alam.
